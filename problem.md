4. Al for national security

Swedish

The company CyberSecurity AB has developed a software that mines the world's open information across social media and other sources to map out actors (individuals and organisations), so as to create and update a gigantic knowledge graph (ontology) to identify targets for adversarial attacks (hospitals, nuclear power plants, politicians, etc.), and to identify potential hostile actors. The company sells services based on this software to other countries for security purposes.

To traverse this graph, they have developed Al agents which are tasked with searching for potential threats, analyzing their activities, predicting their actions, and finding vulnerabilities to fix in organisations and institutions.

Imagine that the Swedish authorities, including the tax agency, employment agency, social insurance agency, and the companies registration authority (Bolagsverket), build together a similar knowledge graph on all businesses, individuals, and transactions, to use for national security purposes. They build Al agents to search the knowledge graph, analyse anomalies, and report to the operator. Furthermore, the agencies also have access to the software by CyberSecurity AB.

Task: you are members of the Swedish government's committee dedicated to developing and regulating the functioning and usage of this system, as well as its interaction with the private, international system by CyberSecurity AB. Outline a policy for the usage of such a system by the Swedish government and assess its operational and ethical risks and opportunities for the state, businesses, and individuals.

Take these questions into account when preparing your presentations and posters:

How should the communication between the agents and operator be shaped to allow the operator to make informed decisions and to act on the information?

Which guardrails would you implement in the system?

How would you try to ensure the reliability of the results of the system?

What ethical and political issues may arise with the use of such a system in Sweden? And on a global scale? How could they be tackled, if at all? what would citizents say?
