Q3 Reliability Assurance

How to ensure reliability of results?

1
Multi-Agent Cross-Validation
GRPO ensemble: agreement → higher confidence, disagreement → automatic human escalation
2
Confidence Calibration
Platt scaling transforms raw LLM confidence into calibrated probabilities. Monthly ECE monitoring.
3
Anti-Hallucination
Every evidence item cryptographically maps to a real database entry. No invented connections.
4
Bias Detection
Quarterly demographic review. Rubber-stamp detection: >95% approval rate → operator retraining.
5
Red Team Testing
Known positives/negatives, adversarial inputs, operator decision quality, disproportion stress tests.
6
Feedback Loops
Closed-without-action cases fed back to reduce false positives of the same pattern.
Reward signal honesty: Unlike code vulnerability detection (objective ground truth), threat assessment is fundamentally subjective — trained on biased human labels. The system cannot be fully autonomous; it requires periodic audit of the training signal itself.

Q4 Ethical & Political Dimensions

What ethical/political issues may arise? What would citizens say?

False Positives
Innocent people flagged and investigated — irreversible harm to reputation and liberty
Mitigation: calibrated confidence + multi-agent validation + right to contest
Chilling Effect
Knowledge of surveillance causes self-censorship, undermining democratic participation
Mitigation: transparency about scope + strict boundary enforcement
Discriminatory Targeting
Proxy variables in data produce biased flagging of ethnic/social groups
Mitigation: G5 disproportion detector + quarterly statistical review
Mission Creep
System scope expands beyond original mandate under political pressure
Mitigation: scope expansion requires months of re-engineering, not a config change
Automation Bias
Operators rubber-stamp AI recommendations without genuine review
Mitigation: evidence-first UI + deliberation delay + engagement tracking
Historical Precedent
IB affair (1973): secret registry targeting unions. FRA-lagen (2008): mass signals intelligence debate.
Sweden's democratic tradition demands strong civilian oversight of any surveillance system

What Citizens Would Demand

  • Transparency — public disclosure of what data is accessed and aggregate outcomes
  • Right to Know — investigated & cleared individuals notified after defined period
  • Right to Contest — legal pathway to challenge AI-informed decisions
  • Sunset Clause — Riksdag renewal every 3 years, not indefinite deployment
  • Independent Oversight — body with full audit access and halt authority

Global Implications

Precedent effect: Strong Swedish governance → positive global standard. Weak governance → legitimizes authoritarian surveillance.

Intelligence sharing: EU/NATO pressure to share findings — audit architecture must support selective, controlled sharing.

CyberSecurity AB: Serves Sweden AND potentially adversarial states simultaneously. This is a structural conflict of interest, not an engineering problem. Architecture must allow full disconnection at any time.
